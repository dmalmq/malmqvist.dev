publishDate: "2026-03-01"
lang: "en"
tags: ["Revit", "PLATEAU", "Digital Twin", "CityGML"]
coverImage: "/images/projects/sta-navi/shinjuku-revit.png"
featured: true
category: Digital Twin
role: "BIM Modeling & Data Pipeline"
context: "JRE Consultants · Tokyo"
timelineLabel: "2024 — Present"
stations: ["Shinjuku", "Shibuya", "Takamatsu", "Ikebukuro", "Sapporo"]
problem: "Tokyo's complex underground station networks are nearly impossible to navigate with traditional 2D maps. PLATEAU, Japan's national 3D city model, provides city-scale digital twin data — but it stops at building exteriors, leaving the labyrinth of underground walkways and station interiors invisible, at some of the busiest transit hubs in the country."
solution: "I create detailed Revit models of station interiors, underground walkways, and surrounding buildings across five JR East stations — Shinjuku, Shibuya, Takamatsu, Ikebukuro, and Sapporo — then convert them to FBX for use as 3D assets in Sta.navi, JR East's indoor navigation app. In parallel, I pipe the same models into CityGML through Blender and Bonsai, integrating them with PLATEAU's digital twin for seamless indoor-outdoor continuity."
techStack: ["Revit", "FBX", "CityGML", "3D Tiles", "Blender", "Bonsai", "RevitGeoSuite"]
impact: "The models now power Sta.navi, JR East's indoor navigation app, across five stations — giving commuters continuous guidance from street level down into the station and back out again at some of Tokyo's busiest transit hubs. The same data feeds PLATEAU's public release, extending Japan's national digital twin below ground, station by station."
