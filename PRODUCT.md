# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is AEC hiring leaders: architecture, BIM, geospatial, and digital-twin hiring managers or technical leads evaluating candidates for specialized roles. They need to understand Daniel’s depth of architectural practice, technical systems capability, direct contribution to complex work, and fit for a role without decoding a generic résumé site.

## Product Purpose

The site exists to win specialized AEC technology roles by turning Daniel’s work into clear, memorable evidence. Success means a qualified hiring leader sees the connection between architectural practice, BIM delivery, code, and operational spatial systems, then starts a focused conversation about a role.

## Positioning

Daniel bridges architectural intent and deployable data: he understands the spatial and delivery logic of architecture, then carries it through BIM, code, and production-ready data pipelines.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: discuss a specialized AEC technology role.
- Secondary CTA: view detailed projects and direct work product.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: architectural intent, deployable data.
- Belief ladder: deep architectural practice → technical systems capability → demonstrated project impact → clear fit for the role.
- Proof on hand: project case studies and work images; interactive technical demos, including the Cesium viewer; English and Japanese résumés; and the career timeline.

## Brand Personality

Bold, experimental, and cinematic. The experience should create confidence in Daniel’s ability to own difficult work, excitement about what his technical range makes possible, and curiosity that rewards deeper exploration. Tengbom is the reference point: architectural credibility led by projects and presented with restraint, while the interaction and storytelling can be more ambitious.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic SaaS landing page. Avoid interchangeable feature-grid structure, pill-heavy component styling, startup framing, and decorative effects that make the portfolio feel like a product template rather than evidence of architectural and technical practice.

## Design Principles

1. Show direct work before making claims about capability.
2. Connect the story in a consistent sequence: practice → systems → impact → fit.
3. Use cinematic interaction to reveal evidence, scale, and process—not to decorate it.
4. Make expert evaluation fast: clarify Daniel’s role, decisions, tools, and outcomes.
5. Treat accessibility as craft; ambition must remain legible, operable, and motion-aware.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain compliant contrast, keyboard access, visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives, meaningful structure and labels, and robust responsive behavior across the multilingual site.
